The Challenge

Students are flying blind with money

Students today use digital payments every day — Google Pay, PhonePe, UPI — but most rarely stop to track where the money actually goes. Without visibility into spending patterns, saving becomes guesswork and building financial discipline feels impossible before it even begins.

01

No Financial Education

Financial literacy is almost never taught in school. Students graduate knowing algebra and history, but not how to budget a monthly allowance, understand interest, or open a savings account. This gap leaves them unprepared for the real financial decisions that begin on day one of college.

02

Untracked Spending

UPI and mobile wallets make spending frictionless — a tap here, a scan there — and most students have no idea how much they've spent until the money is already gone. Without tracking, small daily purchases silently add up into habits that are hard to break later.

03

Zero Savings Culture

Most students live with a "spend now, save later" mindset — assuming savings can wait until they start earning a salary. But the early years are exactly when compound growth has the most impact. Delaying savings by even five years can mean a significant difference in long-term financial outcomes.

04

Investing Feels Intimidating

Existing financial platforms are built for working adults — filled with terminology, complexity, and products that assume prior knowledge. For a student curious about investing, the learning curve feels steep enough to give up before they start. The tools simply weren't designed with them in mind.

✦ Here's our solution
💡

Guided Financial Learning

Finvest makes financial learning part of the everyday experience. Simple, bite-sized resources explain budgeting, saving, and investing in plain language — so students build genuine financial awareness rather than just using tools they don't fully understand.

📱

Automated Expense Tracking

Every transaction paints a picture. Finvest helps students view and analyse their spending in real time — categorised, visualised, and easy to understand. Awareness of where money goes is the first and most powerful step toward changing financial behaviour for the better.

🏆

Goal-Driven Motivation

Abstract advice like "save more" rarely works. Finvest replaces it with tangible, visible goals — a savings bucket with a target, a deadline, and a progress bar. When students can see their goal getting closer every week, saving becomes something they want to do, not something they feel they should.

Market Trends

The rise of student fintech in India

UPI has fundamentally changed how students spend money — over 10 billion transactions happen monthly in India, many made by young adults under 25. Digital payments are no longer new; they are the default. Gen Z is more financially aware than any prior generation, actively seeking out information about investing and financial independence. At the same time, regulators like SEBI are pushing for broader investor education and financial inclusion among youth.

The shift is happening — but the tools haven't caught up. Most fintech products are still built for salaried professionals. Finvest is designed to fill that gap, meeting students where they are and giving them the structure they need to build real financial habits.

Why Financial Literacy Matters

The habit of saving begins at 18

The financial habits formed between ages 18 and 25 have an outsized impact on long-term financial health. A student who starts a small SIP at 18 and stays consistent will accumulate significantly more wealth by 40 than someone who begins at 25 — even with identical income. Early habits compound just as powerfully as money does.

Encouraging financial awareness, budgeting discipline, and goal-based saving during student life doesn't just help students today — it prepares them for every financial decision they will ever make.

Our Story

We were students who got it wrong

Finvest was born out of a simple frustration. Our founders were college students who made every financial mistake in the book — overspending on things they didn't need, saving nothing for months at a time, and watching investment opportunities pass them by because it all felt too complicated. When they looked for tools to help, everything they found was built for working professionals, not students.

So they built what they wished had existed. Finvest's mission is to encourage students to develop financial awareness early — and to approach money with greater confidence, discipline, and long-term thinking. We believe that financial literacy shouldn't begin when you get your first salary. It should begin the moment you start spending.

Frequently asked questions

Is Finvest free for students? +
Yes — Finvest's core features including expense tracking, savings buckets, and financial learning are completely free. Premium features may be introduced in the future.
Is my financial data safe on Finvest? +
Absolutely. Finvest uses bank-grade encryption to protect all user data. We never access or store your account credentials, and we don't share your data with third parties.
Can I link my UPI or bank account? +
UPI and bank account linking is on our roadmap. Currently, students can manually track expenses or import transaction data. Full integration is coming soon.
Do I need a Demat account to use the investment features? +
The Investment Discovery feature is for learning and exploring options only. No Demat account or KYC is required to browse investment opportunities and understand how they work.
Is Finvest available on iOS and Android? +
Finvest is currently available as a web platform. Native iOS and Android apps are in development and will be launching soon. Sign up to be notified first.
How can my college or school partner with Finvest? +
We'd love to work with educational institutions. Reach out to us through the contact form above and our partnerships team will get back to you within 48 hours.
